Did You Know?

Floral fragrances have always been a classic in perfumery, loved for their soft, romantic, and elegant appeal. From ancient Egypt to modern times, flowers like rose, jasmine, and lily have been used in rituals, beauty routines, and fragrance making. In Greece and Rome, floral oils were symbols of love and purity, while in the East, blooms like lotus and tuberose were cherished for their spiritual value. As techniques evolved, especially in the Middle East and Europe, floral notes became essential in perfume blending—either standing alone or paired with fruits, spices, or woods. Today, floral scents remain timeless, offering everything from fresh and delicate to rich and seductive moods in just a single spritz.
